## Why make this public?
"With Aurealis Graphics, I want to publish self- & peer-conducted research in a way that invites other to do the same through open community-led review processes, transparent change histories and a solid pre-established framework.
This LaTeX template being publicized is the first step towards making that happen. The idea stemmed from the incredible amount of novelty and talent within online Minecraft graphics developer communities, and this is my attempt of
facilitating the writing process for the sake of public knowledge." - [@TechDevOnGitHub](https://github.com/TechDevOnGitHub)

## [SOON] How do I get my research published?
1. After finalizing your document, please send your writings to aurealisgraphics@gmail.com for review.

Once reviewed and if applicable to our research fields (real-time computer graphics and adjacent fields), we will encourage you to do the following:

2. Register for an [ORCID](https://orcid.org/) account. This helps us and readers keep track of what works you authored and what fields you research.
   The author link on our website will (by default) lead to your ORCID page.
3. Register for a [Zenodo](https://zenodo.org/) account. This site hosts the final files like the *.pdf* file and *additional resources*. This also makes your work properly citeable and gives it its own unique DOI.
4. After you've logged in to Zenodo with your ORCID or GitHub account and you've uploaded the files there, we can then create a page on [the Aurealis Graphics site](https://aurealis.graphics) to showcase your work.

Preferably, you also host your full latex project as a public repository, for transparency sake. On the website, we will show either the Zenodo version history, or the recent commit history (if available).
